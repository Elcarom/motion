# Community code of conduct

Motion's community is expected to be respectful, technically constructive, and welcoming.

## Expected behavior

- Discuss ideas and code on their merits.
- Give actionable feedback and assume good faith while evidence is incomplete.
- Respect different backgrounds, identities, experience levels, accessibility needs, and communication styles.
- Keep issue reports focused; redact private data and security-sensitive details.
- Credit upstream and third-party work accurately.
- Accept maintainer decisions about scope, safety, release quality, and project direction without harassment.

## Unacceptable behavior

Harassment, threats, hate speech, sexualized attention, doxxing, deliberate intimidation, repeated personal attacks,
spam, impersonation, plagiarism, or publishing another person's private information are not tolerated.

## Enforcement

Repository maintainers may edit or remove content, lock discussions, reject contributions, restrict participation, or
ban accounts when behavior harms the project or its participants. Reports should be sent privately to the repository
owner. Maintainers should protect reporter privacy, document decisions, and avoid conflicts of interest.

This policy applies in repository spaces and in public spaces where someone is representing Motion.
